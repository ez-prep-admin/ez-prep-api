## SOLUTIONS

1. Since an alpha particle and the nucleus both are positively charged, the nucleus will repel the alpha particle. Hence path 3 is not possible.
2. The distance of the closest approach is given by

$$
r_{0}=\frac{1}{4 \pi \varepsilon_{0}} \cdot \frac{2 Z e^{2}}{K}
$$

where $K=\frac{1}{2} m v^{2}$. Thus $r_{0} \propto \frac{1}{K}$. When $K$ is doubled, $r_{0}$ becomes half. Hence the correct choice is (c).
3. The diameter of $n$th orbit is proportional to $n^{2}$, i.e. $d_{n} \propto n^{2}$. Given $d_{1}=1.06 \AA$.
Therefore $d_{10}=(10)^{2} d_{1}=106 \AA$. Hence the correct choice is (d).
4. $\quad E_{\mathrm{n}}=\frac{C Z^{2}}{n^{2}}$. For $n=1$ state of hydrogen, we have $E_{1}=C$ and for the $n$th state of $\mathrm{Li}^{++}$, we have $E_{n}=\frac{C(3)^{2}}{n^{2}}=\frac{9 C}{n^{2}}$. For $E_{1}=E_{n}$, we require $C=\frac{9 C}{n^{2}}$ which gives $n=3$. Hence the correct choice is (c).
5. The first line corresponds to $n=3$.

Therefore,

$$
\begin{aligned}
\frac{1}{\lambda_{1}} & =R\left(\frac{1}{4}-\frac{1}{3^{2}}\right) \\
& =R\left(\frac{1}{4}-\frac{1}{9}\right)=\frac{5 R}{36}
\end{aligned}
$$

or $\lambda_{1}=\frac{36}{5 R}$. We have seen above that $\lambda_{2}=\frac{16}{3 R}$.
Hence

$$
\frac{\lambda_{2}}{\lambda_{1}}=\frac{16}{3 R} \times \frac{5 R}{36}=\frac{20}{27}
$$

or

$$
\lambda_{2}=\frac{20}{27} \lambda_{1}=\frac{20}{27} \lambda
$$

6. The maximum number of electrons allowed in an orbit is $2 n^{2}$. Hence the number of possible elements is

$$
2\left(1^{2}+2^{2}+3^{2}+4^{2}\right)=60
$$

7. In all nuclear reactions, both charge and mass numbers are conserved. Hence the correct choice is (d).
8. Particle $x$ must have zero charge and mass number 1 . Hence it is a neutron.
9. The correct choice is (a). The mass difference is converted into energy in accordance with Einstein's mass-energy equivalence relation $E=m c^{2}$.
10. Statement (b) is wrong.
11. The addition of neutrons to the nucleus of an atom has no effect on the chemical properties.
12. The correct choice is (c). It represents a fusion of four ${ }_{1}^{1} \mathrm{H}$ nuclei with the emission a huge amount of energy.
13. The mass number reduces by $200-168=$ 32. Hence $8 \alpha$-particles are emitted. The emission of $8 \alpha$-particles reduces the atomic number by 16 . But the atomic number reduces by $90-80=10$. Hence the number of $\beta$-particle emitted $=16-10=$ 6. Hence the correct choice is (a).
14. Energy required $=(7.48 \times 13-12 \times 7.68) \mathrm{MeV}=5.08 \mathrm{MeV}$. Hence the correct choice is (d).
15. Energy released in annihilation

$$
=0.51+0.51=1.02 \mathrm{MeV}
$$

Initial energy $=1+1=2 \mathrm{MeV}$. Therefore, the energy of the two photons is $=1.02+ 2=3.02 \mathrm{MeV}$. Hence energy of each photon is $E=1.51 \mathrm{MeV}$. Now, according to DuaneHunt law, the wavelength of a photon of energy $E$ (in eV ) is given by

$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda=\frac{h c}{E \text { in eV }} \AA & =\frac{6.62 \times 10^{-34} \times 3 \times 10^{8}}{1.51 \times 10^{6}} \AA \\
& =8.2 \times 10^{-3} \AA \text { which is choice (c) }
\end{aligned}
$$

16. The distance of closest approach is given by $r_{0}=\frac{1}{4 \pi \varepsilon_{0}} \cdot \frac{2 Z e^{2}}{E_{k}}$. Here $Z=92$ for uranium and $E_{k}=5 \mathrm{MeV}=5 \times 10^{6} \times 1.6 \times 10^{-19} \mathrm{~J}$. Also $e= 1.6 \times 10^{-19} \mathrm{C}$ and $\frac{1}{4 \pi \varepsilon_{0}}=9 \times 10^{9} \mathrm{Nm}^{2} \mathrm{C}^{-2}$. Using these values, we get $r_{0} \simeq 10^{-12} \mathrm{~cm}$, which is choice (c).
17. The binding energies of the reactants and the products in the given nuclear reactions are as follows:

| Reaction | Reactant | Products |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| (a) $Y \rightarrow$ 2Z | $60 \times 8.5 =510 \mathrm{MeV}$ | $2 \times 30 \times 5.0 =300 \mathrm{MeV}$ |
| (b) $\mathrm{W} \rightarrow$ X + Z | $120 \times 7.5 =900 \mathrm{MeV}$ | $(90 \times 8.0 +30 \times 5.0$ ) $=870 \mathrm{MeV}$ |
| (c) $\mathrm{W} \rightarrow$ 2Y | $120 \times 7.5 =900 \mathrm{MeV}$ | $2 \times 60 \times 8.5 =1020 \mathrm{MeV}$ |
| (d) $\mathrm{X} \rightarrow$ Y + Z | $90 \times 8.0 =720 \mathrm{MeV}$ | $(60 \times 8.5 +30 \times 5.0) =660 \mathrm{MeV}$ |

The binding energy of the products in reaction (c) is greater than that of the reactant. Hence reaction (c) releases energy.
18. Beta particle is an electron which is emitted from a nucleus when a neutron decays into a proton and an electron within a nucleus. Hence the correct choice is (c).
19. $\frac{1}{\lambda} \propto Z^{2}$. Since beryllium has highest value of $Z$, it will emit the shortest wavelength. So the correct choice is (d).
20. Before the photon is emitted, the momentum of the hydrogen atom is zero. After the photon is emitted, its momentum
$=\frac{h v}{c}=\frac{E_{3}-E_{1}}{c}$
If $m$ is mass of hydrogen atom and $v$ its
seeoil velocity, then from momentum conservation, we have

$$
\begin{align*}
m v+\frac{E_{3}-E_{1}}{c} & =0 \\
\Rightarrow v & =-\frac{E_{3}-E_{1}}{m c} \tag{1}
\end{align*}
$$

Now

$$
\begin{aligned}
E_{1} & =-13.6 \mathrm{eV} \\
E_{3} & =\frac{-13.6}{\left(3^{2}\right)} \mathrm{eV}=-1.5 \mathrm{eV} \\
E_{3}-E_{1} & =-1.5 \mathrm{eV}-(-13.6 \mathrm{eV}) \\
& =12.1 \mathrm{eV}=12.1 \times\left(1.6 \times 10^{-19}\right) \mathrm{J}
\end{aligned}
$$

Also $m=1.67 \times 10^{-27} \mathrm{~kg}$ and $c=3 \times 10^{8} \mathrm{~ms}^{-1}$. Using these values in (1) and solving, we get $v=-3.86 \mathrm{~ms}^{-1}$. The negative sign indicates that the hydrogen atom recoils after emitting the photon. So the correct choice is (b).
